# Consumer Duty — fairness commentary

This page is the project's differentiator. It explains how the Engine
addresses the FCA's Consumer Duty in the context of climate-driven home
insurance premium changes.

## Regulatory anchors
- **PS22/9** — *A new Consumer Duty (Final rules and guidance)*, FCA, July 2022.
- **FG24/3** — *Final non-Handbook guidance for firms on the Consumer Duty*
  (clarifications and worked examples).
- **PS21/5** — *General Insurance Pricing Practices*, FCA, 2021. The
  loyalty-penalty rules; useful background for fair value in motor and home
  pricing.

URLs are intentionally omitted to avoid stale links — search the FCA
publications hub (https://www.fca.org.uk/publications) by reference.

## Why this matters for climate-adjusted pricing
Climate change shifts the geography of risk. As insurers re-price hazard
exposure with better data, some postcodes — often coastal, often clay-soil,
often historically lower-income — will see steep premium uplifts. Consumer
Duty requires firms to consider:

- **Fair value:** Is the price reasonable relative to the benefit delivered?
- **Avoidable foreseeable harm:** Are predictable harms to vulnerable customers
  being avoided?
- **Customer support:** Are renewals communicated such that the customer can
  act before renewal, not at the door?

## What this project does (Phase 5)
1. Joins postcode-level premium projections to LSOA / Data Zone / NI-equivalent
   IMD data.
2. Computes the distribution of premium uplift (2030 / 2050 / 2070 vs today)
   by IMD decile per nation.
3. Surfaces the result on a public dashboard page so the divergence is visible,
   not hidden inside a model.
4. Documents at least one concrete finding of the form *"decile X sees Y%
   uplift vs decile Z's W% under SSP-N by year M"*.

## What this project does NOT do
- We do not propose pricing remedies (cross-subsidies, caps, signposting).
  Those are regulated decisions for licensed firms and their actuarial /
  compliance functions.
- We do not assert legal compliance with Consumer Duty for any specific firm.
